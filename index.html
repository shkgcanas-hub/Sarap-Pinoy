CHICKEN ADOBO

Prep Time: 15 min
Cook Time: 45 min
Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • ½ cup soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic (minced)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional)
  • 1 cup water
  • Cooking oil

Preparation

In a bowl, combine chicken, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.

In a pan, heat oil and sauté onion and garlic until fragrant. Add marinated chicken and cook until lightly browned.

Pour in the marinade and water. Cover and simmer for 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally until the sauce thickens.

Adjust seasoning to taste. Add brown sugar if desired for a sweeter flavor.

Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy the authentic Filipino flavor!

Note: You can use pork or a combination of pork and chicken for a richer taste!

SINIGANG

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 1 hr
Servings: 4–5

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or ribs (cut into serving pieces)
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 medium tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1 medium onion (quartered)
  • 2 pieces taro (gabi), peeled and quartered
  • 1 radish (sliced)
  • 1 eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 bundle kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2 long green chili peppers
  • 1 pack sinigang mix (tamarind soup base)
  • Fish sauce (to taste)

Preparation

In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add pork, tomatoes, and onions. Simmer for about 45 minutes or until the meat becomes tender.

Add taro and continue simmering until it softens and thickens the broth slightly.

Stir in radish, eggplant, and chili peppers. Cook for 5–7 minutes or until vegetables are tender.

Add the sinigang mix and season with fish sauce to taste. Simmer for another 3–5 minutes.

Finally, add kangkong leaves and cook for another minute before turning off the heat.

Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy the perfect balance of sour and savory flavors!

Note: You can also make sinigang using shrimp, bangus (milkfish), or beef for delicious variations!

KARE-KARE

Prep Time: 25 min
Cook Time: 1 hr 15 min
Servings: 5–6

Ingredients

  • 1 kg oxtail (cut into 2-inch pieces, you can add tripe if desired)
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 tbsp annatto seeds (atsuete)
  • 3 tbsp peanut butter (or ground roasted peanuts)
  • 1 tbsp toasted rice flour (to thicken sauce)
  • 1 medium eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 banana heart (sliced)
  • 1 bundle string beans (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1 bundle pechay (bok choy)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • Cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Shrimp paste (bagoong) — for serving

Preparation

In a large pot, boil oxtail in water for about 1 hour or until tender. Skim off any scum that forms on top. Set aside broth for later use.

In a separate pan, heat oil and add annatto seeds. Stir until oil turns orange, then strain out the seeds.

Using the same annatto oil, sauté garlic and onion until fragrant. Add the boiled oxtail and cook for a few minutes.

Pour in the reserved broth and bring to a simmer. Stir in peanut butter (or ground peanuts) and rice flour. Mix well until the sauce thickens and becomes creamy.

Add the vegetables — start with string beans and banana heart, then add eggplant and pechay. Cook until just tender.

Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot with bagoong (shrimp paste) on the side — the perfect balance to this rich, nutty Filipino classic!

Note: Kare-Kare can also be made with pork hock, beef shank, or seafood for a unique twist!

PORK MENUDO

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 50 min
Servings: 5

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork (shoulder or belly, cut into small cubes)
  • 1/4 kg pork liver (cut into cubes)
  • 2 medium potatoes (diced)
  • 1 carrot (diced)
  • 1 bell pepper (chopped)
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 cups water or pork broth
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • Cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation

In a pan, heat oil and sauté garlic and onion until fragrant. Add tomatoes and cook until softened.

Add the pork cubes and sauté until lightly browned. Pour in soy sauce and vinegar, then let it simmer for 5 minutes without stirring.

Pour in tomato sauce and water or broth. Cover and simmer for about 30 minutes or until the pork becomes tender.

Add the potatoes, carrots, and bell peppers. Continue to cook until vegetables are tender.

Stir in the liver and cook for another 5–7 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and a bit of sugar to balance the flavor.

Serve hot with steamed rice — a colorful, hearty dish perfect for any Filipino gathering!

Note: You can also add green peas or raisins for a touch of sweetness and extra color!
