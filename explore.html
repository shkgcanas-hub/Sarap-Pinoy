ADOBO

Prep Time: 15 min
Cook Time: 45 min
Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • ½ cup soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic (minced)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional)
  • 1 cup water
  • Cooking oil

Preparation

In a bowl, combine chicken, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.

In a pan, heat oil and sauté onion and garlic until fragrant. Add marinated chicken and cook until lightly browned.

Pour in the marinade and water. Cover and simmer for 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally until the sauce thickens.

Adjust seasoning to taste. Add brown sugar if desired for a sweeter flavor.

Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy the authentic Filipino flavor!

Note: You can use pork or a combination of pork and chicken for a richer taste!

PORK SINIGANG

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 1 hr
Servings: 4–5

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or ribs (cut into serving pieces)
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 medium tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1 medium onion (quartered)
  • 2 pieces taro (gabi), peeled and quartered
  • 1 radish (sliced)
  • 1 eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 bundle kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2 long green chili peppers
  • 1 pack sinigang mix (tamarind soup base)
  • Fish sauce (to taste)

Preparation

In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add pork, tomatoes, and onions. Simmer for about 45 minutes or until the meat becomes tender.

Add taro and continue simmering until it softens and thickens the broth slightly.

Stir in radish, eggplant, and chili peppers. Cook for 5–7 minutes or until vegetables are tender.

Add the sinigang mix and season with fish sauce to taste. Simmer for another 3–5 minutes.

Finally, add kangkong leaves and cook for another minute before turning off the heat.

Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy the perfect balance of sour and savory flavors!

Note: You can also make sinigang using shrimp, bangus (milkfish), or beef for delicious variations!

KARE-KARE

Prep Time: 25 min
Cook Time: 1 hr 15 min
Servings: 5–6

Ingredients

  • 1 kg oxtail (cut into 2-inch pieces, you can add tripe if desired)
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 tbsp annatto seeds (atsuete)
  • 3 tbsp peanut butter (or ground roasted peanuts)
  • 1 tbsp toasted rice flour (to thicken sauce)
  • 1 medium eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 banana heart (sliced)
  • 1 bundle string beans (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1 bundle pechay (bok choy)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • Cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Shrimp paste (bagoong) — for serving

Preparation

In a large pot, boil oxtail in water for about 1 hour or until tender. Skim off any scum that forms on top. Set aside broth for later use.

In a separate pan, heat oil and add annatto seeds. Stir until oil turns orange, then strain out the seeds.

Using the same annatto oil, sauté garlic and onion until fragrant. Add the boiled oxtail and cook for a few minutes.

Pour in the reserved broth and bring to a simmer. Stir in peanut butter (or ground peanuts) and rice flour. Mix well until the sauce thickens and becomes creamy.

Add the vegetables — start with string beans and banana heart, then add eggplant and pechay. Cook until just tender.

Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot with bagoong (shrimp paste) on the side — the perfect balance to this rich, nutty Filipino classic!

Note: Kare-Kare can also be made with pork hock, beef shank, or seafood for a unique twist!

PORK MENUDO

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 50 min
Servings: 5

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork (shoulder or belly, cut into small cubes)
  • 1/4 kg pork liver (cut into cubes)
  • 2 medium potatoes (diced)
  • 1 carrot (diced)
  • 1 bell pepper (chopped)
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 cups water or pork broth
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • Cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation

In a pan, heat oil and sauté garlic and onion until fragrant. Add tomatoes and cook until softened.

Add the pork cubes and sauté until lightly browned. Pour in soy sauce and vinegar, then let it simmer for 5 minutes without stirring.

Pour in tomato sauce and water or broth. Cover and simmer for about 30 minutes or until the pork becomes tender.

Add the potatoes, carrots, and bell peppers. Continue to cook until vegetables are tender.

Stir in the liver and cook for another 5–7 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and a bit of sugar to balance the flavor.

Serve hot with steamed rice — a colorful, hearty dish perfect for any Filipino gathering!

Note: You can also add green peas or raisins for a touch of sweetness and extra color!

LUMPIA (FILIPINO SPRING ROLLS)

Prep Time: 25 min
Cook Time: 30 min
Servings: 8–10 rolls

Ingredients

  • ½ kg ground pork or beef
  • 1 medium carrot (grated)
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 cup cabbage (finely shredded)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 10 lumpia wrappers
  • Cooking oil (for frying)

Preparation

In a large bowl, mix ground pork, carrot, onion, garlic, cabbage, egg, soy sauce, salt, and pepper until well combined.

Lay a lumpia wrapper on a flat surface. Spoon about 1–2 tablespoons of the filling near one edge, then roll tightly, folding in the sides to seal the filling.

Brush a bit of water or beaten egg on the edge of the wrapper to seal it properly.

In a pan, heat enough oil for deep-frying. Fry lumpia rolls in batches over medium heat until golden brown and crisp.

Drain excess oil using paper towels and serve hot with sweet chili sauce or vinegar-garlic dip!

Note: For a vegetarian version, you can replace the meat with tofu and more vegetables — equally delicious and crispy!

LECHON KAWALI

Prep Time: 20 min
Cook Time: 1 hr
Servings: 4–5

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly (liempo)
  • 6 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • 1 medium onion (quartered)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • Water (enough to cover the pork)
  • Cooking oil (for deep frying)

Preparation

In a large pot, combine pork belly, garlic, onion, bay leaves, peppercorns, and salt. Add enough water to cover the meat and bring to a boil.

Simmer for 40–50 minutes or until the pork is tender. Remove the pork and let it cool completely.

Once cooled, pat the pork dry with paper towels. For extra crispiness, refrigerate it uncovered for a few hours or overnight.

Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Carefully fry the pork belly until golden and crispy. Flip occasionally to cook all sides evenly.

Drain on paper towels and let it rest before chopping into serving pieces. Serve with lechon sauce or spiced vinegar.

Note: For a crunchier texture, you can double-fry the pork — just make sure to let it rest between each fry to maintain its juiciness!

TINOLA

Prep Time: 15 min
Cook Time: 40 min
Servings: 4–5

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger (sliced thinly)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 4 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 small green papaya or sayote (sliced)
  • 2 cups chili leaves (dahon ng sili) or malunggay leaves
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil

Preparation

Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.

Add the chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned on all sides.

Pour in water or chicken broth and fish sauce. Bring to a boil, then lower heat and simmer for 25–30 minutes or until the chicken is tender.

Add the papaya or sayote and continue cooking until the vegetables are soft.

Season with salt and pepper to taste. Add the chili or malunggay leaves and simmer for another minute.

Serve hot with steamed rice — perfect for a cozy, rainy day meal!

Note: For extra flavor, you can add a bit of lemongrass or substitute green papaya with sayote if preferred.
